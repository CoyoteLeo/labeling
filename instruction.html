Background

There are two articles followed by multiple pairs of short paragraphs (most fewer than 3 sentences) from the articles. The two articles are labeled as "Recalled Paragraph" and "Retold Paragraph" with events from the two found below in bolded text. The goal is to label the pair of paragraphs as either "Match" or "Conflict" depending on whether or not the meanings of the two are consistent.

Annotation

Steps
  1. Read the original articles.
  2. Read the paragraphs.
  3. Connect the events with the correct label based on the information from the articles and paragraphs.
    PS: Just drag the dot near the source event to the dot near the target event and the connection will be built automatically.
    PS: If you want to remove the connection, you can click the "X" button near any end of the connection, it'll remove all connections about that dot.
  4. After going through all pairs using the "Next" button, click "Submit."

Relation Label
Match: The event from both the Recalled Paragraph and the Retold Paragraph describe the same thing, and there are no conflicts between each other (i.e. they can use different words, but they express the same meanings). Please mind that they're the same things, which means that they must occur at the same timing. That is, any kinds of causation will NOT be seen as a MATCH. The following are some examples:
Recalled event Retold event Relation Reason
Our family, consisting of, 8 - year old son. we, have, an 8 year old Match different description, but same meaning
We, proceeded to, our hotel. We, reaching, our hotel on the Canadian side Match the second is more detailed, but both of them have same meaning.
1. he, asked, me
2. he, asked, marry him 		he, propose Match Each recalled/retold event may matched to multiple retold/recalled events.
PS:
he, asked, me
  • he → subject
  • asked → predicate
  • me → object
Conflict: In contrast to "Match", "Conflict" means the events describe the same event (also at the same timing), but it's obviously inconsistent. Moreover, the inconsistences not only occur at Predicate, but could also occur at Subject or Object. Here are some examples:
Recalled event Retold event Relation Reason
No one, plays, sports you, do, sports Conflict Same event, but their meanings conflict.
I, walk to, school I, drive to, school Conflict Same event, but their meanings conflict.
he, helped, me 1. My mother, help up, me
2. I, told, her		 Conflict - recalled: my brother was staying with me and he came quickly and helped me
- retold: My mother came from out of her room to attend to me, trying to help me up at first, but when I screamed in pain after she pulled my arm, I just told her to call for help.

The event at the same timing, but their subjects conflict.
(No relation): If there are no relations between the events, you can leave it empty and continue on to the next pair.
In other words, you don't have to link the dots between the recalled and retold events.
P.S. : Some relations must be seen as (no relations). Such as Casuation, Sequence ctions, etc.
